Mike Schur

Michael Schur is an American television producer, writer, and actor. He is most well known for his work on network comedies such as The Office, Parks and Recreation and Brookln Nine-Nine. His most recent venture is on the show The Good Place.

  • Mike Schur was born in Ann Arbor, Michigan, the city where Ann Perkins and Chris Traeger move to in season 6 of Parks and Rec.
  • Mike and his casting directors like to use the same actors in other shows they work on and can immediately recall if someone will be right for a role
  • Mike Schur plays the role of Dwight’s cousin Mose in The Office, and hated playing the role. Though he liked Mose, the costume was generally made of wool and he would be seen as Mose outdoors in the sun most of the time, and though Mose had lines written for him, they would often get cut out. They thought it was funnier if he didn't say anything. “It was brutal,” Schur said.

Parks and Recreation

Parks and Rec. is an American television sitcom that aired on NBC from 2009 to 2015. It began as a spin-off of The Office, but was reworked to create the Parks and Rec. we know now.

  • One of Mike Schur’s favorite lines from filming Parks was one that the writer’s didn’t even have in the script. It was an ad-libbed line from Chris Pratt in the episode “title name” where he suggests Leslie may have “network connectivity problems” after searching her flu-like symptoms online.
  • Ben Schwartz would not cut his hair for as long as possible before playing the role of Jean Ralphio on Parks, in order to make him look as ridiculous as possible according to Schwartz.
  • Ron Swanson's trait of not wanting anyone to know when his birtday is, was taken from one of the shows actual producers, Morgan Sackett. While filming Parks, Mike Schur and Dean Holland got Sackett a cake for his birthday, began to sing happy birthday to him and Sackett attacked Holland and threw the cake on the ground.

The Office

The Office is an American television sitcom that aired on NBC from 2005 to 2013. It was adapted from a British show of the same name, created by Ricky Gervais.

  • Phyllis Smith was working as a casting associate for the show and they ended up creating the role of Phyllis Vance specially for her.
  • In season 4, episode 3, there is a scene where Meredith asks Jim to sign her cast. Instead of signing as Jim Halpert, John Krasinski signed his real name on her cast.
  • Many of the scenes in the opening credits were actually shot by John Krasinski.
  • Angela Kinsey and Jenna Fischer, who play Angela Martin and Pam Beesley on the show, are actually good friends in real life.

Sources

Brooklyn Nine-Nine

Brooklyn Nine-Nine is an American television sitcom that aired on FOX from 2013 to 2018. After the show got cancelled by FOX in May, it was quickly picked up for another season by NBC, which will begin in 2019.

  • Andy Samberg and Chelsea Peretti, who play Jake Peralta and Gina Linetti respectively, have been friends in real life since they were kids. Chelsea even revealed on a late night show that she had a crush on Andy for a while when they were younger.
  • Melissa Fumero, who plays Amy Santiago, was pregnant for much of filming season 3. Because of this, they used several ways to hide her baby bump, like large purses, holding papers infront of her and even pretending to be someone pregnant in one episode.
  • Marc Evan Jackson, who now plays Kevin on B99, was almost casted to play Charles Boyle, but they casted Joe Lo Truglio instead.

Sources

The Good Place

The Good Place is an American television sitcom that has been on air with NBC since 2016. The show features Emmy-award-winning actor, Ted Danson.

  • NBC approached Mike Schur with blank air time for a new show that they wanted Schur to pitch and produce. He could make it anything he wanted and what he came up with was The Good Place.
  • Jameela Jamil, who plays Tahani Al-Jamil, was not an actress before The Good Place, but instead worked as a BBC introducer. Her first audition ever was for the role of Tahani and she only went to the audition so she could potentially meet Mike Schur.
  • Fans discovered that Parks and Rec. and The Good Place were in the same universe because of magazine ads. In various magazines that Eleanor reads, there are advertisements for products by Dennis Feinstein and Jean-Ralphio Saperstein, both characters from Parks.
  • The fake curse words was not initially a part of the good place, it was created to avoid TCC regulations and perfectly fell into place. They have had no issues with the fake words.
  • The entire staff worked hard to keep the plot twist at the end of season one a secret. Actors kept the secret from their spouses, writers kept the secret from guest directors and they all had to pretend that there was no twist ending when promoting the show in panels and on TV. Everyone tried their best to make the secret a shock to all viewers except Ted Danson. Danson knew the twist all along and revealed after the twist episode aired that he had given away the secret to tons of people because he wanted to prove that the show was going to get really good.

Master of None

Master of None is an American television series and Netflix original that was released for streaming in 2015. The show is currently on hiatus after two seasons.

  • Mike Schur currently acts as executive producer for Master of None.
  • Master of None was filmed to resemble a movie rather than a TV series in order to set the show apart from other shows being produced.
  • Dev's parents in Master of None are Aziz Ansari's actual parents. Ansari had been struggling to find two Indian actors that were right for the roles, so he decided to hire the two people he knew could do it best.
